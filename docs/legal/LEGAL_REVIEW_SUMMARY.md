## WHAT EACH DOCUMENT DOES

### 1. Terms of Service
**Who signs:** ALL users when creating an account  
**When:** First login/registration  
**Purpose:** Platform-wide rules and liability protection

**Key Protections:**
- ✅ OutMeets is a PLATFORM ONLY (not event organiser)
- ✅ No liability for injuries, damages, or losses
- ✅ Users assume all risks of participation
- ✅ Indemnification (users pay our legal costs if they sue)
- ✅ Dispute resolution and arbitration clauses
- ✅ Intellectual property protection

**Critical Clauses:**
- Section 5: "OutMeets is NOT LIABLE for any injuries, damages, or losses"
- Section 7: "You agree to INDEMNIFY and HOLD HARMLESS OutMeets"
- Section 6: "You VOLUNTARILY ASSUME ALL RISKS"

---

### 2. Privacy Policy
**Who signs:** ALL users when creating an account  
**When:** First login/registration  
**Purpose:** GDPR compliance and data transparency

**Key Features:**
- ✅ GDPR-compliant (UK & EU)
- ✅ Clear data collection disclosure
- ✅ User rights (access, deletion, portability)
- ✅ Third-party data sharing transparency
- ✅ Cookie usage disclosure
- ✅ Data retention policies

**Data Collected:**
- Email, display name, profile photo
- Event registrations and group memberships
- Comments and interactions
- IP address (for rate limiting)
- Google OAuth data (if used)

**User Rights:**
- Right to access data
- Right to deletion ("right to be forgotten")
- Right to data portability
- Right to object to processing
- Right to lodge complaint with ICO

---

### 3. Organiser Agreement
**Who signs:** Users creating their FIRST group or event  
**When:** Before creating first group/event  
**Purpose:** Establish organiser responsibilities and liability

**Key Responsibilities:**
- ✅ Organiser is INDEPENDENT (not employee of OutMeets)
- ✅ Organiser is PERSONALLY LIABLE for participant safety
- ✅ Duty of care to participants
- ✅ Risk assessment and emergency preparedness
- ✅ Insurance requirements (£5M+ public liability recommended)
- ✅ Accurate event information and risk disclosure

**Insurance Requirements:**
- **Public Liability:** £5,000,000+ (STRONGLY RECOMMENDED)
- **Personal Accident:** £100,000+
- **Professional Indemnity:** £1,000,000+ (if charging fees)

**Prohibited Activities:**
- Leading activities beyond competence
- Misrepresenting qualifications
- Organising while intoxicated
- Endangering participants
- Discrimination or harassment

**Emergency Procedures:**
- Must carry first aid kit
- Must carry communication device
- Must have emergency contacts
- Must report serious incidents to OutMeets

---

### 4. Event Participation Waiver
**Who signs:** Participants joining EACH event  
**When:** Before joining each event  
**Purpose:** Event-specific liability release

**Key Protections:**
- ✅ Acknowledgment of inherent risks (falls, weather, wildlife, etc.)
- ✅ Voluntary participation and assumption of risk
- ✅ Release of OutMeets from ALL liability
- ✅ Release of Organiser from liability (except gross negligence)
- ✅ Indemnification clause
- ✅ Medical fitness representation

**Risks Disclosed:**
- Environmental: Falls, weather, terrain, wildlife, getting lost
- Physical: Exhaustion, injuries, medical emergencies
- Equipment: Gear failure, inadequate supplies
- Human: Organiser decisions, other participants, own actions

**Additional Data Collected:**
- Emergency contact (name, phone, relationship)
- Medical conditions (optional but recommended)
- Medications and allergies
- Blood type (optional)

**Medical Treatment Consent:**
- Authorizes organiser to administer first aid
- Authorizes calling emergency services
- Participant responsible for medical expenses

---

### 5. Cookie Policy
**Who signs:** ALL visitors to website  
**When:** First page load (cookie banner)  
**Purpose:** GDPR/ePrivacy Directive compliance

**Cookie Categories:**
1. **Essential Cookies** (always active, no consent needed)
   - Authentication tokens (JWT)
   - Session management
   - CSRF protection

2. **Functional Cookies** (optional, requires consent)
   - User preferences
   - Return URL for seamless login
   - Search history

3. **Analytics Cookies** (optional, not currently implemented)
   - Google Analytics (if added in future)

4. **Marketing Cookies** (not used)

**User Controls:**
- Cookie consent banner with 3 options
- "Accept All" / "Essential Only" / "Customize"
- Can change preferences anytime in footer
- Browser controls explained
- Do Not Track (DNT) respected

## LEGAL RISK ASSESSMENT

### HIGH RISK (Requires Strong Protection)

#### 1. Participant Injury or Death
**Risk:** Participant injured or killed during hiking event  
**Likelihood:** MEDIUM (hiking inherently dangerous)  
**Impact:** CATASTROPHIC (£100K+ lawsuit, reputation damage)

**Protections in Place:**
- ✅ Event Participation Waiver (releases OutMeets & Organiser)
- ✅ Assumption of risk clauses
- ✅ Clear risk disclosure requirements
- ✅ Organiser duty of care obligations
- ✅ Emergency contact collection
- ✅ Incident reporting system

**Remaining Risk:** LOW (if waiver enforced by courts)

---

#### 2. Organiser Negligence Lawsuit
**Risk:** Participant sues claiming organiser was negligent  
**Likelihood:** MEDIUM (organisers may lack qualifications)  
**Impact:** HIGH (could name OutMeets as co-defendant)

**Protections in Place:**
- ✅ Organiser Agreement establishes independence
- ✅ Platform NOT liable for organiser actions
- ✅ Indemnification (organiser pays our legal costs)
- ✅ Insurance recommendations (£5M+ public liability)
- ✅ Qualification disclosure requirements
- ✅ Clear duty of care obligations

**Remaining Risk:** LOW-MEDIUM (depends on court interpretation)

---

#### 3. GDPR Violation / Data Breach
**Risk:** Data breach or GDPR non-compliance  
**Likelihood:** LOW (good security practices)

- ✅ Comprehensive Privacy Policy
- ✅ Cookie consent banner
- ✅ User rights (access, deletion, portability)
- ✅ Data retention policies
- ✅ Third-party data processing agreements

## SOLICITOR QUESTIONS

When meeting with your solicitor, ask:

### Liability & Waivers
1. Are our liability limitations enforceable in UK courts?
2. Is the waiver language strong enough for hiking activities?
3. Can we limit liability for organiser negligence?
4. What happens if a participant is under 18?
5. Do we need different waivers for different activity types?

### Insurance
6. What insurance coverage do we need as a platform?
7. What insurance should we REQUIRE from organisers?
8. Should we verify insurance before allowing event creation?
9. What happens if an organiser's insurance lapses?

### Regulatory Compliance
10. Do we need Adventure Activities Licensing?
11. Are there Health & Safety regulations we must follow?
12. Do we need to register with any regulatory bodies?
13. What are our obligations for incident reporting?

### GDPR & Data Protection
14. Is our Privacy Policy GDPR-compliant?
15. Do we need a Data Protection Officer (DPO)?
16. How long should we retain legal agreement data?
17. What are our obligations for data breaches?

### Platform Operations
18. Should we require organisers to have specific qualifications?
19. Can we ban organisers who receive safety complaints?
20. What's our liability if we fail to enforce our own terms?

## RED FLAGS TO WATCH FOR

### High-Risk Organisers
- No hiking experience or qualifications
- Multiple safety complaints
- No insurance coverage
- Misrepresenting event difficulty
- Ignoring weather warnings

**Action:** Suspend organiser account, investigate, potentially ban

### High-Risk Events
- Extreme difficulty (Expert level)
- Remote locations (no mobile signal)
- Multi-day expeditions
- Winter/snow conditions
- Water crossings or climbing

**Action:** Require additional waivers, insurance verification, qualification checks

### Legal Complaints
- Participant injury claims
- GDPR complaints
- Discrimination allegations
- Insurance disputes
- Waiver challenges

**Action:** Document everything, notify solicitor, preserve evidence, cooperate with authorities
