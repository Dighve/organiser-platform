## 1. ACKNOWLEDGMENT OF RISKS

### 1.1 Inherent Dangers of Outdoor Activities

I understand and acknowledge that hiking and outdoor activities involve **INHERENT RISKS AND DANGERS** that may result in serious injury or death, including but not limited to:

#### Environmental Hazards:
- **Falls:** Slips, trips, and falls on uneven terrain, rocks, roots, ice, or wet surfaces
- **Weather:** Extreme heat, cold, rain, snow, lightning, high winds, fog
- **Terrain:** Steep slopes, cliffs, loose rocks, river crossings, avalanche zones
- **Wildlife:** Encounters with dangerous animals (bears, snakes, insects, etc.)
- **Natural disasters:** Landslides, rockfalls, flash floods, earthquakes
- **Getting lost:** Disorientation, navigation errors, separation from group
- **Altitude:** Altitude sickness, reduced oxygen at high elevations

#### Physical & Medical Risks:
- **Exhaustion:** Physical fatigue, dehydration, heat stroke, hypothermia
- **Injuries:** Sprains, fractures, cuts, bruises, blisters, muscle strains
- **Medical emergencies:** Heart attack, stroke, allergic reactions, asthma attacks
- **Pre-existing conditions:** Aggravation of existing medical conditions
- **Lack of medical facilities:** Remote locations with limited access to emergency care

#### Equipment & Gear Risks:
- **Equipment failure:** Boots, clothing, backpacks, trekking poles
- **Inadequate gear:** Insufficient clothing, food, water, or emergency supplies
- **User error:** Improper use of equipment or navigation tools

#### Human Factors:
- **Organiser decisions:** Route choices, pace, timing, weather assessment
- **Other participants:** Actions or negligence of other group members
- **My own actions:** My own negligence, poor judgment, or physical limitations
- **Communication:** Misunderstandings, language barriers, unclear instructions
