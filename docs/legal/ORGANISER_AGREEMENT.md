## 2. ORGANISER QUALIFICATIONS & RESPONSIBILITIES

### 2.1 Minimum Requirements
As an Organiser, you represent and warrant that you:
- Are at least **18 years old**
- Have the legal capacity to enter into this agreement
- Possess adequate knowledge and experience for the activities you organise
- Are physically and mentally fit to lead outdoor activities
- Have no criminal convictions that would make you unsuitable to lead groups

### 2.2 Hiking-Specific Qualifications (Recommended)
While NOT legally required, we STRONGLY RECOMMEND you have:
- **First Aid certification** (Wilderness First Aid or equivalent)
- **Navigation skills** (map reading, compass, GPS)
- **Experience** leading groups in similar terrain and conditions
- **Emergency response training**
- **Knowledge** of local trails, weather patterns, and hazards

**IMPORTANT:** OutMeets does NOT verify your qualifications. Participants rely on your honesty.

### 2.3 Duty of Care
You owe a **DUTY OF CARE** to all participants. This includes:
- **Risk assessment:** Evaluate hazards before and during events
- **Route planning:** Choose appropriate trails for group ability
- **Weather monitoring:** Cancel or modify events in dangerous conditions
- **Participant screening:** Ensure participants are fit for the activity
- **Emergency preparedness:** Carry first aid kit, emergency contacts, communication device
- **Group management:** Keep group together, monitor for signs of distress
- **Clear communication:** Provide accurate event descriptions and requirements

### 2.4 Prohibited Activities
As an Organiser, you must NOT:
- Lead activities beyond your competence
- Misrepresent your qualifications or experience
- Organise events while under the influence of alcohol or drugs
- Discriminate against participants based on protected characteristics
- Engage in sexual harassment or inappropriate conduct
- Endanger participants through reckless behavior
- Use the Platform for commercial gain without OutMeets' permission

### 4.2 OutMeets' Limited Role
**OutMeets is NOT LIABLE** for:
- Your actions or decisions as an Organiser
- Injuries or damages arising from your events
- Your failure to obtain insurance
- Your violation of laws or regulations

### 4.3 Insurance Requirements

#### Mandatory Insurance (STRONGLY RECOMMENDED)
You MUST obtain adequate insurance coverage, including:

**Public Liability Insurance:**
- Minimum coverage: **£5,000,000** (UK) or equivalent
- Covers injuries to participants and third parties
- Covers property damage
- Available from outdoor activity insurers

**Personal Accident Insurance:**
- Covers your own injuries while leading events
- Recommended: £100,000+ coverage

**Professional Indemnity Insurance (if charging fees):**
- Covers claims of negligence or breach of duty
- Recommended: £1,000,000+ coverage

#### Where to Get Insurance
- **British Mountaineering Council (BMC):** https://www.thebmc.co.uk/insurance
- **Ramblers Association:** https://www.ramblers.org.uk
- **Outdoor activity insurers:** Search "hiking group leader insurance UK"

#### Proof of Insurance
- You may be required to provide proof of insurance
- Upload insurance certificate to your organiser profile (future feature)
- Participants have the right to request proof

You agree to **INDEMNIFY and HOLD HARMLESS** OutMeets, its officers, directors, employees, and agents from:

## 8. EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

### 8.1 Emergency Preparedness
Before every event, you must:
- Carry a **first aid kit** (appropriate for group size and activity)
- Carry a **communication device** (mobile phone, satellite messenger)
- Have **emergency contacts** for all participants
- Know the location of nearest **emergency services** (hospital, mountain rescue)
- Carry **emergency shelter** (space blankets, bivvy bag)
- Have a **contingency plan** (escape routes, bad weather plan)

### 8.2 During an Emergency
If an injury or emergency occurs:
1. **Assess the situation:** Is it life-threatening?
2. **Call emergency services:** 999 (UK), 112 (EU), or local equivalent
3. **Provide first aid:** Within your training and competence
4. **Keep group safe:** Move to safe location if needed
5. **Document incident:** Take notes, photos (if appropriate)
6. **Notify OutMeets:** Email safety@outmeets.com within 24 hours

### 8.3 Incident Reporting
You must report to OutMeets:
- **Serious injuries** requiring medical attention
- **Deaths** (immediate notification)
- **Near misses** that could have caused serious harm
- **Participant complaints** about safety

**How:** Email safety@outmeets.com with:
- Event name and date
- Description of incident
- Injuries sustained
- Actions taken
- Outcome

### 8.4 Mountain Rescue & Emergency Services
In the UK:
- **Mountain Rescue:** 999 or 112, ask for Police then Mountain Rescue
- **Air Ambulance:** Dispatched by emergency services
- **What3Words:** Use to provide exact location (download app)
